January 2020

Monthly report

VAG Rework

The total VAG rework is in progress. For now Volkswagen brand is in work. The primary goal of the rework is to add all the Engines according to official brochures with the accurate Model Codes, rewriting the rules using Model and Engine Codes. This process is quite time consuming, however, it would benefit our website a lot, especially when we have more markets and users with the VAG cars with international engines.

For now Volkswagen Golf MK7 and Golf MK7 Facelift is in work. The rules are already rewritten according to the engine and model codes. However, there are still some international engines that we are adding to have 100% accurate data. You may check the rules structure here. Fortunately, for the latest Golf generations there is some logic in model codes, so we may use the standard pattern for it. (in comparison, we had to write down all the possible model codes for the older generations, which is quite long and boring :p )

In addition, we are starting quite ambitious project inside the VAG rework. As soon as Alona Franko was trained in AMR for this particular rework, we are going to start Polo and Passat at the same time. We are expecting that the rework will double its` speeb :)

GM brands overhaul

(Chapter 1 - Chevrolet)

Chevrolet is one of the crown jewel and an icing on GM's cake. Moreover, it's the largest American brands that features hell a lot of regional subsidiaries with completely different lineups across those. I like Canadian Chevrolet subsidiary the most, since it features pretty different lineup of series and sub-series which is drastically different to what American Chevrolet offered. However, Chevrolet is a key to the whole GM-brands, since the lifespan patterns, chassis and engines are shared among the whole range of brands constituting GM per se. Thus having Chevrolet covered means at least some template for other brands.

Just as usual, you can check overall progress here. I've included a new sheet with table legend for better convenience. Yellow cells denote tier-2 (manily LADM) series, which will be added as soon as the priority ones are done. I'm currently right on track on track with retrofits being implemented. The progress is severely hampered by crappy Transport Styrelsen VIN-code data (they are prone to misplasing crucial VIN-symbols or truncating the whole segments of VIN), thus, besides proper VIN-based AMR-rules, I'm forced to create monstrous fallback AMR-rules (I call those tier-2 rules) relying on model name field in order to cover those idents whose VIN data is completely screwed up. In addition, there's a rule of a thumb while dealing with American cars: don't ever forget about Canadian market and fleet orders. I've lost some some time having loosened my grips on this rule applying Chevelle retrofits. I was forced to go back and rewise some generation in order for those to incorporate Canadian lineups and police special models.

I've been heavily involved with Chevrolet mid-size lineup retrofits this month. Chevy Intermediates feature mind-blowing lineup of subseries and late model-year performance variations additions. I had to cross-check numerous sources to come up with proper lineup of both Chevelles and El Caminos. The model years comprising a generation have been turned into standalone generations. The following intermediate series have been completely covered: Chevelle and El Camino.

As a result the number of unconnected idents has decreased by further ~15%. We are on the brink of breaching 450 unconnected idents threshold. I'm planning on covering non full-size Chevrolet series remaining, i.e. Corvair, Chevy II, Canaro and Corvette and swithinng on to full-sizers. In addition I need to research on and cover Canadian non full-sizers by GM based on corresponding Chevy models, i.e. Acadian and Beaumont cars.

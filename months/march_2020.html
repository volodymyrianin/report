GM brands overhaul

(Chapter 1 - Chevrolet)

Chevrolet is one of the crown jewel and an icing on GM's cake. Moreover, it's the largest American brands that features hell a lot of regional subsidiaries with completely different lineups across those. I like Canadian Chevrolet subsidiary the most, since it features pretty different lineup of series and sub-series which is drastically different to what American Chevrolet offered. However, Chevrolet is a key to the whole GM-brands, since the lifespan patterns, chassis and engines are shared among the whole range of brands constituting GM per se. Thus having Chevrolet covered means at least some template for other brands.

Just as usual, you can check overall progress here. I've included a new sheet with table legend for better convenience. Yellow cells denote tier-2 (manily LADM) series, which will be added as soon as the priority ones are done. As of now, I'm finalizing all the revamps on Chevrolet brand. 1957 model year is being currently processed and it's the last one to be retrofitted.

    What have been done?
  • 1956-1966 Chevrolet full size series have been covered, including: 150, 210, Delray, Biscayne, Bel Air, Impala.
  • Minor tweaks were applied to: Honda Odyssey RL6 Facelift, latest Chevy Traverse, ABT Transporter.
  • Complete overhaul of Toyota Mark X
  • Complete overhaul of Toyota HiAce H10
  • Four batches of Patrick Leone's images and ~30 feedbacks were handled.
  • Overdrive transmission issue research.

    Plans for the next month:
  • Some final brushes to the whole Chevrolet brand including checking up what idents would remain unmatched.
  • Final revision of Chevrolet G-Van.
  • Switching on to full-blown GMC brand-wise retrofits.
  • Overdrive transmission fix

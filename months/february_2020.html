February 2020

Monthly report

VAG Rework

The total VAG rework is in progress. For now Volkswagen brand is in work. The primary goal of the rework is to add all the Engines according to official brochures with the accurate Model Codes, rewriting the rules using Model and Engine Codes. This process is quite time consuming, however, it would benefit our website a lot, especially when we have more markets and users with the VAG cars with international engines.

    For now the following models of VW were revamped
  • All generations of Golf
  • Polo IV (Type 9N)
  • Polo IV (Type 9N3)
  • Passat B6

GM brands overhaul

(Chapter 1 - Chevrolet)

Chevrolet is one of the crown jewel and an icing on GM's cake. Moreover, it's the largest American brands that features hell a lot of regional subsidiaries with completely different lineups across those. I like Canadian Chevrolet subsidiary the most, since it features pretty different lineup of series and sub-series which is drastically different to what American Chevrolet offered. However, Chevrolet is a key to the whole GM-brands, since the lifespan patterns, chassis and engines are shared among the whole range of brands constituting GM per se. Thus having Chevrolet covered means at least some template for other brands.

Just as usual, you can check overall progress here. I've included a new sheet with table legend for better convenience. Yellow cells denote tier-2 (manily LADM) series, which will be added as soon as the priority ones are done. As of now, I'm currently a bit ahead of schedule with retrofits being implemented. There are only 11 model years of Chevrolet full-size series (i.e. 1956-1966, to be more precise) to be covered. The good thing is there are no major differences between American and Canadian GM lineup during this time span, thus at least one usual hurdle can be disregarded. The bad thing is that 1956-1964 VIN codes do not provide any particularily useful information in terms of chassis and engines, hence there's going to be even more fallback rules.

  • All Chevy non-fullsizers have been covered within the span of February.
      The following series have been utterly overhauled
    • Corvair
    • Chevy II
    • Camaro
    • Corvette
  • Chevy II and Nova series were separated into standalone series of their own in order to maintain consistency across all GM brands.
  • Canadian GM subsidiaries have been completely covered as well, i.e. Acadian (Canadian variation of Chevrolet Nova) and Beaumont (Canadian Chevrolet Chevelle). Such cars are very-very rare and sought after, nevertheless one Beaumont found its way to Sweden somehow. I found it in the list of idents with unspecified (i.e. Ã–vriga) brand value.
  • 1967-1970 Chevrolet full size series have been covered as well. It includes Biscayne,Bel Air,Impala,Caprice and corresponding wagon variations of these. The wagon variation has been merged with their corresponding regular series counterparts in order to maintain consistensy across GM brands and, moreover, to unclog Chevy's brand page, because it already seems a bit overcrowded. There had been an issue with Chevrolet having 2 concurrect coupe styles within their full-size lineup and, as an aftermath both these styles were matched to one shared entity. It should not be a case any more.
  • As a caveat, there appear to be around a dozen of military Chevrolet C/K vehicles called CUCV M1008 (Chevrolet K30 variation) and CUCV M1009 (a rugged Chevrolet K5 Blazer). I decided to include these, because we already have got military LR Defenders and MB G-class cars. Plus, it would help to differentiate proper CUCVs from replicas.
  • Toyota Soarer lineup has been retrofitted in accordance with Japanese sources as well. We were lacking engines for all the generation of Soarer apart from Z30.

    Plans for the next month:
  • Finishing with overhaul of Chevrolet full-size series remaining.
  • Some final brushes to the whole Chevrolet brand including checking up what idents would remain unmatched (I've seen a Brazilian Chevrolet Bonanza among unconnected idents) and adding some regional series we're missing.
  • Final revision of Chevrolet G-Van. It appears so that there was a conversion van chassis even for earlier generations. I overlooked this fact due to lack of correct information regarding these even in official GM sources and I've got to rectify this mishap as well.

Specifications Maintenance

In general, all the tasks related to specifications are perfomed well. The 150 list is updated after each ident import and it's followed by some of the employees.

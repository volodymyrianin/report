American Market Overhaul

American cars are different, so is the whole American market in general. There are distinctive differences with European cars in terms of almost everything: be it appearance, life cycles, VIN codes, safety standards, equipment etc. Chevrolet brand has been completely overhauled, now it's time to apply the same patterns to the rest of American brands and series. This time I'll stick to platform-by-platform approach across all the brands including badge-engineered series.

Just as usual, you can check overall progress here. I've conducted roadmaps for GM Truck & MPV and (partially) GM Passenger cars. Same roadmaps will be done for other manufacturers as well. As of now, I'm working on GMT610-derived vans (i.e. Chevy Express and GMC Savana). 18 model years have been completely overhauled including specs.

    What have been done? Miscellaneous final brushes to varios Chevrolet series. More details here. These include:
  • Engine codes have been standardized across European Chevrolet lineup in accordance with recently discovered new source.
  • Overdrive-related issue across pre 1967 Chevrolet lineup has been eliminated.
  • Ordinary Automatic transmissions were replaced with Hydra-Matic ones in accordance with latest MDS transmission data.
  • Chevrolet Tracker series has been completely overhauled.
  • Chevrolet P-Series has been created.
  • GMC Savana G610 pre-facelift has been completely overhauled (full-specs included).
  • GMC Savana G610 facelift is being overhauled right now.

    Plans for the next month:
  • Chevrolet/GMC GMT600 vans full-stack overhaul.
  • Ford Econoline/E-Series 4th Generation (1992-...) full-stack overhaul.
  • Dodge Ram Wagon 3rd Generation (1994-2003) full-stack overhaul.

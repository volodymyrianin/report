December 2019

Monthly report

VAG rework

We are working with VAG almost for the 6 months but still we are only in the beginning of our journey. However, the most difficult model in Volkswagen is almost done. VAG rework is quite a complex task involving a lot of cross brand/model work, meaning that changes in Volkswagen Golf may affect Audi models :p In addition, we are talking about the hundreds of thousands connections that are not always connected to right engines. The reason for it is different engines for different markets. So it is quite important to finish this task to avoid any negative feedbacks related to connections and other issues (like engine volume in cm3, torque which could be different for the engines with the same Volume in L and power output)

During December we did not start any rewritings because our main focus was on altering the existing rules (to bring their quality to some kind of perfection ;) ) WE lowered the number of unconnected idents for Golf and solved all cross model conflicts that were caused due to the same vin codes for Golf and Jetta models. Among the generations that have been rechecked there are the following: MK1 Facelift, MK2, MK3, MK3 Facelift, MK4, MK5

During January we are going to work with Golf MK6 and MK7. As for the first one, the model codes are already gathered and engines were already added / changed. As for the second one, we are going to do the whole circle of rework.

In Juanuary we hope to finish Golf all generations and start working with othe Volkswagen models.

GM brands overhaul

(Chapter 1 - Chevrolet)

Chevrolet is one of the biggest American brands that features hell a lot of regional subsidiaries with completely different lineups across those. Plus, tideous license agreements with numerous third-party auto makers make handling Chevrolet pretty sophisticated. However, Chevrolet is a key to the whole GM-brands, since the lifespan patterns, chassis and engines are applicable the the whole range of brands constituting GM per se. Thus having Chevrolet covered means at least some template for other brands. Blanditiis eum dolores reiciendis!

I've retroffitted the whole Chevrolet lineup from 1970s-1980s, including Camaro, complete full-size and mid-size range and Nova-based series. I'm merging back wagon series into their corresponding passenger vehicle ranges in order to simplify and standardize series representation, i.e. Kingswood Estate has been moved to Caprice, Kingswood to Impala, Townsmans to Bel Air and Parkwood to Biscayne. Furthermore, I've covered Canadian lineup of Chevrolet cars from 1970s as well. There are some Canadian specific powerplant-chassis combination which we have not covered up until now. Not many idents were influenced (example, this Canadian Chevrolet Chevelle Malibu Sport Sedan equipped with 6-cylinder Turbo-Thrift 250 engine), I must say, but it's quite unique info nobody really covers anywhere else across the web. I'm working with my all-time favorite Chevrolet series right now (i.e. 1968-1972 Chevelle, including El Caminos). I will have finised this one by next Tuesday. As a result the number of unconnected idents has decreased by further ~20%. We are on the brink of breaching 500 unconnected idents threshold.

Just as usual, you can check overall progress here. I've included a new sheet with table legend for better convenience. Yellow cells denote tier-2 (manily LADM) series, which will be added as soon as the priority ones are done. I'm currently on track with retrofits being implemented slightly a bit ahead of my schedule. The progress is severely hampered by crappy Transport Styrelsen VIN-code data (they are prone to misplasing crucial VIN-symbols or truncating the whole segments of VIN), thus, besides proper VIN-based AMR-rules, I'm forced to crete monstrous fallback AMR-rules (I call those tier-2 rules) which are based on model field in order to cover those idents whose VIN data is completely screwed up.

I'm planning on covering Chevrolet series from 1960s, and, probably, if I manage to push a bit harder and the data on those is not that bad, the remaining ones from 1950-ish model years. In addition, I'm going to start AMR- and creation training for selected number of guys. Right now I'm conducting a visual guide a la " Creation How-To" and "AMR How-To".
